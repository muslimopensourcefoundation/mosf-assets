Volunteer Role Title:
Uganda Field Coordinator (Volunteer)
Reports to:
Founder & Executive Director, Muslim Open Source Foundation
Commitment:
5–10 hours per week (flexible, hybrid in-person and remote, volunteer basis)
Overview
The Muslim Open Source Foundation (MOSF) is a newly forming nonprofit based in Washington State, USA, dedicated to empowering the global Muslim community through open-source collaboration, education, and ethical technology.
As a Uganda Field Coordinator (Volunteer), you will help stand up the pilot MOSF × AUDF Tech Lab in Kampala, support local training delivery, and ensure reliable ICT infrastructure and transparent documentation. You will coordinate between MOSF’s remote team and AUDF’s on-the-ground staff, helping translate open-source curricula into practical, sustainable learning for youth and women.
This is a volunteer position offering the chance to contribute meaningfully from MOSF’s earliest stages and grow alongside its mission.
Key Responsibilities
* Technical Setup and Support
   * Assist AUDF with lab setup in Kampala, including computers, networking, internet access, and basic power considerations.
   * Perform on-site troubleshooting, maintenance, and inventory tracking of ICT equipment.
   * Document configurations, checklists, and procedures in MOSF repositories and shared workspaces.
* Training Enablement
   * Support delivery of digital literacy and introductory programming sessions using open-source curricula.
   * Help adapt materials for local context and languages where possible.
   * Encourage responsible and ethical technology use aligned with Islamic values.
* Coordination and Reporting
   * Serve as the point of contact between MOSF and AUDF for pilot planning, logistics, and weekly updates.
   * Capture progress notes, photos, and simple metrics for transparency reports and website updates.
   * Join regular coordination calls with MOSF and share risks, needs, and recommendations.
* Community Representation
   * Represent MOSF professionally with learners, trainers, partners, and local institutions.
   * Help identify and onboard additional local volunteers or trainers as the program grows.
Ideal Candidate
* Diploma or higher in ICT, Computer Science, or related field.
* Hands-on experience with computer labs, basic networking, and end-user support.
* Comfortable coordinating across teams and documenting work clearly.
* Passion for Islamic values, education, and service to community.
* Based in Uganda and able to be present in Kampala as needed. Willingness to travel within Uganda if required for outreach or expansion.
Benefits
* Contribute to a mission-driven global-local initiative from its earliest phase.
* Learn and practice open-source collaboration and transparent reporting.
* Recognition on the MOSF website as an inaugural volunteer contributor.
* Letter of recommendation and potential for future leadership as the program scales, insha Allah.
