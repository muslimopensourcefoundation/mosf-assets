Volunteer Role Title:
Backend Engineer (Volunteer)
Reports to:
Founder & Executive Director, Muslim Open Source Foundation
Commitment:
5–10 hours per week (flexible, remote, volunteer basis)
Overview
The Muslim Open Source Foundation (MOSF) is a nonprofit based in Washington State, USA, dedicated to empowering the global Muslim community through open-source collaboration, education, and ethical technology.
As a Backend Engineer (Volunteer), you’ll play a key role in laying the technical foundations of MOSF’s early open-source initiatives. You’ll help design and develop backend systems that support educational and collaborative tools, establish reusable service templates for future projects, and contribute to a growing ecosystem that unites Islamic knowledge with modern software engineering.
This is a volunteer position offering the opportunity to contribute meaningfully from MOSF’s earliest stages and grow alongside its mission.
Key Responsibilities
Backend Development & Infrastructure
* Develop and maintain backend services, APIs, and integrations for MOSF’s early projects (e.g., educational platforms, repository tools, or community resources).
* Establish coding standards, documentation practices, and starter templates for future MOSF open-source repositories.
* Contribute to multilingual support, authentication systems, and integration with web frontends.
* Collaborate on database design, API specifications, and deployment workflows.
Collaboration & Mentorship
* Work closely with the Founder and other volunteers to translate project goals into technical deliverables.
* Participate in code reviews and help define best practices for ethical, transparent open-source development.
* Support onboarding of future volunteer developers as the team grows.
Automation & Tooling
* Contribute to scripts or services that automate internal processes, such as open-source project evaluation or content management.
* Explore opportunities to use modern backend frameworks and deployment strategies that balance simplicity, scalability, and accessibility.
Ideal Candidate
* Proficient in one or more modern backend stacks (e.g., .NET C#, Python, Go, Ruby, or TypeScript/Node.js).
* Familiar with relational databases (MSSQL, PostgreSQL, MySQL) and common ORM frameworks.
* Comfortable with Git-based collaboration, Docker, and cloud deployment environments.
* Passionate about Islamic values, education, and ethical technology.
* Organized, reliable, and proactive in asynchronous remote environments.
* Excited to help shape the technical standards of a mission-driven nonprofit from the ground up.
Benefits
* Direct mentorship and collaboration with the Founder & Executive Director.
* Opportunity to design foundational systems that will support global Muslim open-source initiatives.
* Recognition on the MOSF website as an inaugural volunteer contributor.
* Letter of recommendation and potential for future leadership or paid roles post-funding, insha Allah.
